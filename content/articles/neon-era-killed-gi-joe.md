The conventional story goes like this: Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles arrived in 1988 and stole G.I. Joe's crown. Power Rangers finished the job in 1993. External competition killed the franchise.

That's not what happened.

Go look at the G.I. Joe figures from 1985 and compare them to 1991. In 1985 you had Firefly, a realistic Cobra saboteur in gray camo. In 1991 you had the Eco-Warriors, figures that changed color when you sprayed them with water. In neon green.

The gritty military aesthetic that made G.I. Joe iconic was replaced by toy gimmicks. Color-changing paint. Spring-loaded weapons. Figures in electric purple and fluorescent orange. Cobra went from a menacing terrorist organization to a parade of wacky villains.

The competition mattered, sure. But G.I. Joe's biggest enemy was the people making G.I. Joe.
