Real name: Robert "Bob" Remus. By day, a professional wrestler in the WWF. By 1985, a G.I. Joe character with his own action figure, cartoon appearances, and toy commercials.

Sgt. Slaughter is the only real, living person to ever become a G.I. Joe. He voiced himself in the animated series. He appeared in TV commercials alongside the toys. He had multiple figure versions released due to sustained demand (Renegades, Slaughter's Marauders).

In the 1980s, the line between entertainment categories was blurrier than it is today. A wrestler could be a cartoon hero could be an action figure could be a commercial spokesman. All at once. All canonically the same character.

That kind of crossover simply doesn't happen anymore. IP lawyers killed the fun.
