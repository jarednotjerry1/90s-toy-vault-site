Snake Eyes first appeared in the very first wave of the Real American Hero line in 1982. All black. No face. No voice. Literally mute in the lore, scarred and disfigured in Vietnam.

He shouldn't have worked. A character with no face, no dialogue, and no personality file card beyond classified information. But something about the mystery made him magnetic.

His rivalry with Storm Shadow, a Cobra ninja who was actually his childhood friend, became the most iconic relationship in the franchise. Two former students of the same ninja master, torn apart by tragedy and manipulation.

A near-mint straight-arm 1982 Snake Eyes sold at Heritage in 2021 for **$26,400**. The most expensive 3.75-inch G.I. Joe figure ever auctioned.
