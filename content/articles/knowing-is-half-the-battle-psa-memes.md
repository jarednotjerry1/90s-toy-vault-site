"And knowing is half the battle."

If you grew up watching G.I. Joe in the 1980s, you heard that line hundreds of times. Every episode of the 1983 to 1986 cartoon ended with a public service announcement. A kid would get into trouble, a Joe would show up with advice, and the kid would say "Now I know!"

It became one of the most quoted catchphrases of the decade. But the story didn't end there.

In 2003, filmmaker Eric Fensler took the original PSA segments and dubbed completely nonsensical dialogue over them. "Porkchop sandwiches!" "Body massage!" "Who wants a body massage?" These became some of the earliest viral videos, spreading across eBaum's World and early YouTube.

The Fensler Films PSAs are a defining artifact of early internet humor. They proved that remix culture could turn corporate earnestness into comedy gold.
