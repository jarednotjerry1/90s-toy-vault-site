Seven feet six inches long. Three feet wide. Almost three feet tall.

The USS Flagg aircraft carrier is the largest action figure playset ever produced. By anyone. Ever. And according to "The Toys That Made Us" documentary, it was never supposed to be a real product.

The Flagg was designed as a convention display piece. A showroom model meant to impress retailers at Toy Fair. Somewhere in the approval chain at Hasbro, someone greenlit it for mass production by mistake. And instead of pulling it back, they just went with it.

The retail price was $109.99 in 1985. Adjusted for inflation, that's roughly $315 today. For a toy. A seven-foot toy that most kids didn't have room for.

Today, a complete boxed version sells for $3,000 to $5,000 or more. Even incomplete ones fetch $1,250 and up. The accidental toy became a grail.
